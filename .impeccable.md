## Design Context

### Users
This dashboard is used by people managing a Home Assistant driven home, often from a wall tablet, laptop, or phone. They need fast situational awareness and low-friction control over safety, comfort, and energy systems without hunting through menus.

### Brand Personality
Clear, confident, composed. The interface should feel trustworthy and premium, with a practical Scandinavian smart-home character rather than playful gadget styling.

### Aesthetic Direction
Favor a dark, high-contrast control-room feel with strong hierarchy, crisp labels, and motion that explains state. Safety-critical surfaces such as alarm controls may use sharper warning tones like ember orange and alert red, but the overall system should still feel calm and deliberate. Avoid toy-like UI, generic glassmorphism, and noisy decoration.

### Design Principles
1. Safety status must be understandable at a glance.
2. Motion should explain countdowns, transitions, and urgency, never distract from them.
3. Controls with real-world consequences must feel explicit and hard to misread.
4. Cards should adapt cleanly across compact mobile layouts and larger tablet dashboards.
5. Reduced-motion users should keep the same clarity without relying on animation.

### Notes
This context is based on the current dashboard, repository documentation, and the current request for a more expressive Alarmo card. Refine it further if product-specific brand guidance becomes available.
